# family_leave
Maven Analytics family leave challenge with Power bi tool.

Link to Project : 

Dataset : https://www.mavenanalytics.io/blog/maven-family-leave-challenge  
Steps : Data Cleaning  
Removing Duplicate records.  
Splitting Industry column by “:” delimiter and making sub industry column.  
Replacing N/A values in Paid Maternity Leave, Unpaid Maternity Leave, Paid Paternity Leave and Unpaid Paternity Leave with 0.  
Changing data-type of Paid Maternity Leave, Unpaid Maternity Leave, Paid Paternity Leave and Unpaid Paternity Leave columns to decimal for future calculations.  
Creating Custom column Total Leave Paid and Total Leave Unpaid.  
Creating Dax measures: Total Company, Total Industry, Total Maternity Leave Paid, Total Paternity Leave Unpaid, Average Maternity Leave Paid and Average Paternity Leave Paid.  
Using Different visualization tools like bar chart, pie-chart and treemap. Showing major KPI using cards.  

![image](https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/123319398/232176419-ae4c1d8d-2375-464a-83c3-c9e2168717aa.png)


Insights:  
Average Maternity leave paid is 10.91 weeks, while Average Paternity leave paid is 1.32 weeks only.  
The highest average maternity leave paid is provided in Finance Industry at 13.33 week.  

It is found out that approximately 64% Maternity leave is paid and 36% is unpaid.  
Software sub-industry is at the top position which gives maternity paid leave at 38%.  
Dynatrace and Grant Thornton company offered the best maternity leave paid at 52 and 51 respectively.  

Total Paternity leave paid is 2,115 and unpaid is 495, which is 18.9%. 
Computer Software Industry gives 6 weeks paid paternity leave on average and sits at the top position.  
Dropbox company provides the highest paid paternity leave at 24 weeks, followed by Medidata Solutions at 16 weeks.  
Among the sub-industries, Consumer market provides the fairest paid paternity leave at 28%.
